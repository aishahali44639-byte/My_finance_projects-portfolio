Executive Summary

India's electric vehicle market crossed a decisive inflection point in FY2025–26, with 2.55 million EVs sold — a 25% year-on-year increase. EV penetration reached 8.3% of total vehicle registrations, up from 7.5% the previous year, while the overall automotive market grew 13.2% to 30.84 million vehicles. The EV market is growing nearly four times faster than the broader auto industry, with a 52% CAGR over the past five years.

7. ⚠️ Key Challenges & Risks

Charging Infrastructure Bottleneck

30,000 chargers on paper → 14,450 operational. Utilization below 10%. Fragmented apps – consumers need multiple apps to access different charging networks.

Battery Manufacturing Gap

PLI ACC scheme has achieved only 2.8% of its 50 GWh target. Ola Electric faces ₹12.5 lakh/day penalties for delays. India remains heavily dependent on imported lithium‑ion cells, primarily from China.

Profitability Crisis

Most EV companies are burning cash. Subsidies for E2W and E3W are being phased out from March 2026.

Subsidy Cliff (March 2026)

The biggest near‑term risk is the end of subsidies for two‑ and three‑wheelers. Manufacturers will need to prove they can compete on pricing and scale rather than policy support.

Rare Earth Metals Shortage

Chinese export restrictions on rare earth metals disrupted India's EV industry in FY26, affecting production.

9. 💡 Investment Themes & Conclusion

Thesis: India's EV ecosystem has crossed the critical inflection point from policy‑driven adoption to market‑driven growth. However, the transition will be bumpy – requiring massive investments in charging infrastructure, battery manufacturing, and supply chain localization.

Key Investment Themes

  • Charging Infrastructure – ₹2,000 Cr allocated for 72,000 chargers
  • Battery Manufacturing – PLI ACC scheme, domestic cell production
  • Commercial EV Adoption – buses, trucks, logistics
  • Component Localization – ₹41,000 Cr → ₹3,02,000 Cr by 2032
  • Consolidation Play – weaker players will exit or merge

Companies to Watch

  • Tata Motors – market leader, positive EBITDA
  • Mahindra & Mahindra – 407% growth in FY26
  • Olectra Greentech – consistent revenue growth
  • TVS Motor – E2W market leader
  • Bajaj Auto – top PLI beneficiary

Red Flags

  • Subsidy phaseout (March 2026)
  • Battery manufacturing delays – 97.2% of PLI ACC target unmet
  • Charging infrastructure gap – only 14,450 of 30,000+ chargers operational
  • Rising losses – Ola, MG Motor burning cash
  • Rare earth metals dependency
